Mechanic of the day — Spell Parry Clash

Controls
Mage: move, jump, spell, blink. Fighter: move, jump, slash, parry/guard dash.
Rules
Fighter parry has 8 active frames; if it catches a projectile, freeze for 6 frames, palette-flash both players, then reflect a weaker bolt.
Win condition
First to 3 crystal breaks. Mage wins by spacing traps; fighter wins by closing distance and converting reflected spells.
What to test
Can spectators tell: “projectile launched → parried → reflected → punish window” in under one second?

Juice checklist

  • Use two palette lanes: cyan/purple for mage intent, gold/red for fighter threat, white-only for impact.
  • Add 3-frame hitstop on blocked hits, 6-frame hitstop on parries, 10-frame hitstop on crystal break.
  • Make projectiles leave square particles that inherit team color, then flip color when reflected.
  • Screen shake should be horizontal for sword hits, radial pulse for spells, vertical thump for platform landings.
  • UI clarity: tiny mana pips under mage, guard pips under fighter, and a center “CLASH!” stamp only on successful parry.
  • Marketing micro-goal: record a 9-second loop that shows one read, one parry, one comeback KO.
